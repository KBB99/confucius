Lu Xun100 years old. And still too right?

When all the world is mad, sanity is the worst form of madness.

At the start of the 20th century the efficiency that capitalism created was becoming palbable.As a result all the societies that embraced capitalism quickly began to outpace everyone else. When Lu Xun was young, China was not only holding onto its outdated traditions but also taking great pride in doing so. In desperation Lu Xun searched for something else and found the Western system. But when he attempted to share his newly found knowledge with China he was met with indifference. It seemed that things would go on as they always had.

"I finally glimpsed what lay between every line, of every book: Eat People!" Lu Xun.

When I flick through the history books, I find no dates, only those fine Confucian principles benevolence, righteousness, morality snaking their way across each page. As I studied them again, through one of my more implacably sleepless nights, I finally glimpsed what lay between every line, of every book: Eat People! All these words written in books, spoken by the farmer stare strangely, smirkingly at me. Are they planning to eat me, too?

In this short excerpt from Diary of a Madman Lu Xun summarizes the damage that accompanies every ideology. As the madman realizes, since ancient times everyone has been following tradition. Even the farmer, someone who has never even glanced at formal Confucian teachings, lives by its principles. The madman, filled with terror, realizes that he will be unable to escape and will soon have to choose between joining their ranks and being eaten.

"Ah-Q" A Passenger in Life

To further illustrate the damage blind faith causes, and more specifically the faith Confucian values that Gu admires so very much, let us turn to another story of Lu Xun, The Real Story of Ah-Q. With satire on every page, Lu pains a picture of the life of Ah-Q. What most stands out is the fact that although Ah-Q is never in control of his destiny, he lives with an enormous amount of pride, constantly performing mental acrobatics to reaffirm his superiority, convinced that he is truly in control. The audience on the other hand, is fully aware of what is truly driving Ah-Qs life.

As a result, Ah-Q is a passenger in his own life, rationalizing as best he can everything that occurs to him. This story is also a criticism geared towards traditional China. Since the Opium Wars China had been a passenger in world history, not capable of making any decisions, but nevertheless suffering due to the decisions of everyone else. Astoundingly, China retained its sense of superiority, just like Ah-Q. Of course Ah-Q had to die, for his values never changed.

Thanks to Lu Xun and other revolutionaries, China was able to reconsider its values and realign them to work rationally rather than habitually. Soon, a new China arose that continued developing until the 21st century, becoming the most important player in world events. Such is the power of revolution.
